Brief Preface:

In the United States, no one is required by law to vote in any local, state, or presidential election.
According to the U.S. Constitution, voting is a right and a privilege.
While many constitutional amendments have been ratified since the first election,
none of them made voting mandatory for U.S. citizens.

Even though voting is a right and a privilege - to participate in the election process of your local, state,
and federal government, there are a few basic requirements that must be met...

The Voter Registration Requirements for The United States of America are as follows:

  • To register you must be a citizen of the United States

  • To vote on state issue the State's residency requirements must be met.
    This can vary by state. (You can be homeless and still meet these requirements.)

  • Voter must be 18 years old on or before Election Day. (Pre-registration ages vary by state,
    but you must be 18 years old to vote and in some state you must be 18 before the election)

  • It is the sole responsibility of the voter fulfill all of the requirements of the state before the specified deadline.
    (If you don't register in time, you don't get to vote!)

The Voter Registration Age Verification Tool will verify that the registrant is at least 18 years old, based on the user input,
and then alert the registrant if they meet the required minimum age criteria and are able to vote according to the calculation.
