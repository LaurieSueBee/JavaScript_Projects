Is it time to schedule your patient's yearly eye exam? --

This is a demo of a new tool available for immediate use in Optometry Clinics :

Introducing...
The Function amEyeLate();
and its counterpart function... amEyeLateProtocol()


This function will take the date of your patient's last eye exam and project a year forward to when the patient's next eye exam is due.

It will notify the office staff a month ahead of time and prompt the staff to take action and mail a reminder with a 15% off coupon to the patient.

It will also take into consideration that if the action is not handled before 2:00pm of that business day it will not be reflected as handled until the following business day (due to the time needed to process the reminder and get it ready to for the postal service).

Finally, the function will instruct the staff to follow up with the patient 14 days after the reminder was mailed . This will provide further assistance to the patient in case they need help with scheduling the yearly Eye Exam. This is a courtesy call to the patients from which no reply has been received after 14 days.

The implementation of these procedures should increase the number of recurring yearly eye exam and in turn, increase the percentage of doctors whose practices are in compliance with the preventative wellness check best practice recommendations as recently passed by the National Board of Optometry.

Protocol:


The previous preemptive task module (Protocol Section) informs staff of the recommended procedure for the successful implementation of Best Practice Procedures. The goal is to increase the overall health and well-being of patients through preventive medicine( timely wellness check-ups). These Check-ups will also strengthen doctor / patient relations by making sure patients don't wait too long in between visits. This form of preventive medicine allows the optometrist to be a valuable resource for primary Physicians, Internal Medicine Practitioners, or any other healthcare provider the patient reports to. The info obtained during these wellness checks should be shared with the patient's whole "team of healthcare providers". This open-ness will help with the early detection of potential health risks and quicker treatment to remedy these issues, if there are any to address. Through education, kindness, compassion,mutual respect and teamwork; preventive medicine will serve as a powerful tool in the early detection of disease or any change in a patient's health markers, while also bridging the communication gap between the patient and all members of the health care team that have been honored with the privilege of providing their care




THE MOST IMPORTANT THING IS THIS :
Timely wellness checks, the foundation blocks of preventive medicine, are key to early detection and treatment of many major health issues.

A person's eye can reveal much more to a physician than simply the quality of their vision...

Here is a list of health issues that can be detected from an eye exam:

  • Diabetes: If a doctor looks into your eyes and sees hemorrhaging, you may have diabetes

  • High blood pressure: Torn or bent eye blood vessels could show high blood pressure

  • Arthritis: Inflammation of the sclera or uvea could signal arthritis

  • Cancer: Abnormal eye structure could signal cancer

  • Tumors: Pupils that have developed an irregular shape could be a tumor

  • High Cholesterol: Yellow appearance around the cornea or plaque filled retina blood vessels may show high cholesterol

  • Thyroid disease: Bulging eyes can be a sign of thyroid issues

  • Autoimmune diseases: Inflammation of the optic nerve could be a sign of multiple sclerosis or lupus

  • Blood clotting disorder: Optic nerve swelling could signal an issue with blood clotting

  • Some recent research has even started showing that eye exams can provide early diagnosis of autism in children.
